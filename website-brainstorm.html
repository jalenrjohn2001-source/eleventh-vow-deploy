Key design decisions on this page

  • Hero is aspirational, not transactional. "Every dream venue" before "Bid on auctions" — emotional language first, mechanic second. Couples buy on emotion.
  • The word "auction" appears only after the dream is sold. Sub-headline introduces the concept, not the headline. Same logic Patrick applied to the venue pitch.
  • Ending soon section creates urgency without manufacturing scarcity. Real countdown on real auctions. Scarcity that's earned, not invented.
  • How It Works is the demystifier. First-time visitors don't trust the auction format yet. Three steps in plain language removes the anxiety before the bid.
  • "Deposit refundable if the tour falls short" is the most important sentence on the page. Solves the offline-booking trust problem in one line.

Key design decisions on this page

  • YTD Revenue is the first number they see. Reminds them why they're here. Lift percentage right next to it tells the success story.
  • Bidder Insights is a primary sidebar item, not buried. Even when a venue's auction doesn't lift much, the bidder data has standalone value — they take it back to their direct sales team to inform fixed pricing.

  • Calendar view as a sidebar item. Venues think in dates. Give them a calendar-first view of upcoming events rather than a flat list.
05 · Design System Pointers

The visual language that ties it all together.

Premium hospitality aesthetic — closer to Aman or Soho House than to The Knot or WeddingWire. The aesthetic is intentional brand positioning, not decoration. Premium look = premium venue association.

Core Visual Language

Color Palette

Midnight navy as the brand anchor. Champagne gold as the singular accent. Cream as the breath. Restraint is the look.

#0A1729
#C9A961
#FBF8F3
#E8C5BD

Avoid more than one accent color per page. Restraint signals premium.

Typography

Playfair Display serif for all headlines, page titles, venue names, prices. Editorial feel.

Inter sans-serif for body, UI, data, navigation. Modern, clean, readable on every device.

Generous letter spacing on uppercase labels (0.12em–0.20em). Tabular figures for all numeric data — currency aligns, bids align, no jagged digits.

Voice & Tone

Couple-facing: aspirational, emotional, specific. "Your wedding" not "a wedding." Use sensory language for venue descriptions.

Venue-facing: clear, financial, respectful. No fluff. Lead with the dollar value to the venue, not the platform features.

Never use the word "discount" anywhere. Replace with "exclusive rate," "curated mid-week," or "premier opening."

Imagery Standards

Real venue photography only — no stock. Each listing needs minimum 8 photos including: hero exterior, ceremony space, reception space, suite, food/styling detail.

Photo curation is part of the platform's promise. Build photo standards into the venue onboarding checklist.

Couples respond to lifestyle photography — actual weddings happening, not staged spaces. Build a "real weddings" library from past clients.

06 · UX Considerations Specific to This Business

The five things wedding auction platforms have to get right.

1. The trust ladder before the bid

  • Couples won't bid blind. Every auction page needs verified photos, video walkthrough where possible, real reviews from past weddings at that venue, and clear deposit-protection language directly under the bid button.
  • The platform should display a "verified venue" badge with the criteria explained on hover — minimum years in business, liability insurance confirmed, photos verified, contract on file.

2. Auto-bid is non-negotiable

  • The "set your maximum and we'll bid up to it" mechanic eliminates auction sniping anxiety. Couples set a ceiling once and walk away. They check back when they get the win/loss email.
  • Without auto-bid, couples either over-pay in the heat of the moment or under-bid out of risk aversion. Both kill the platform's value proposition.

3. The 72-hour close window matters more than the listing window

  • Listings can be live for weeks, but the bidding window — when couples can actually bid — should be a tight 48–72 hours per auction. Compressed urgency drives bid intensity. Patrick's house-sale FOMO logic applied directly.
  • Show the countdown timer prominently. The clock is the conversion engine.

4. The post-win flow is critical and underbuilt elsewhere

  • The 60 seconds after a couple wins is the highest-conversion moment in the entire customer journey. Built-in upsell prompts: "Need a photographer? See our recommended partners. Book by Friday and get 10% off." This is where Money Models upsells activate.
  • Send a win confirmation email within 30 seconds of the auction closing. The longer the gap, the colder the lead becomes.

5. The venue's listing creator should write the listing for them

  • Most venue owners can't write good marketing copy. The listing creator should be a wizard: upload photos, fill out structured facts, AI-generate a draft description in the Eleventh Vow voice, venue reviews and approves with light edits.
  • This dramatically reduces venue onboarding friction. A venue can list a date in 15 minutes instead of 90.

6. Photo experience innovations — the differentiator (Brainstorm Update · May 22)

  • Time-of-day slider on hero photography. Drag from morning → afternoon → golden hour → reception. Same venue, four different sales pitches depending on the couple's vision.
  • Real weddings toggle. Switch between staged venue-only shots and actual past weddings held at the venue. Couples want to project themselves into the space — empty ballrooms are real estate listings, not wedding inspiration.
  • Mood chips. Boho · Candlelit · Modern · Garden · Romantic. Photo grid re-sorts to lead with images matching the selected vibes. Same database, personalized order.
  • Hero reel autoplays on hover. 12-second cinematic edit generated via Higgs Field. Galleries are dead; reels are how Gen Z evaluates everything.
  • Why these matter: The Knot's photo gallery is functionally identical to a real estate listing. Eleventh Vow's job is to make a venue page feel like a story about a wedding day, not a directory entry. These features are the wedge — the auction is the mechanic, the photo experience is what makes couples pick the platform.

7. The post-win celebration sequence (Brainstorm Update · May 22)

  • On-site: Full-screen confetti animation. "YOU JUST BOOKED [VENUE]" hero moment. Auto-generated share-to-Instagram card (couple name + venue + date in Eleventh Vow brand). Free distribution.
  • Email #1 (within 30 seconds): Celebration-first. Subject line does the work. Logistics deferred.
  • Email #2 (1 hour later): Logistics — venue contact, contract handoff, next steps.
  • Email #3 (24-48 hours later): The meme email. Wedding-themed funny content. Lands when adrenaline has settled and they're showing friends. Top-of-mind without sales-pitch tone.
  • The outbid recovery flow: "Outbid — here are 3 similar venues closing this week" within 5 minutes of close. Couples who lost still proved real intent. Most valuable warm-lead pool the platform has — don't let them bounce to The Knot.

8. Couple-side marketing language reframe (Brainstorm Update · May 22)

  • "Bidding" carries eBay/sneaker-collector baggage that clashes with the once-in-a-lifetime emotional weight of choosing a wedding venue. On couple-facing marketing surfaces, prefer "live offers," "couples compete for your date," or "open offers."
  • Inside the product (dashboard, bid panel), "bid" is fine — it describes the actual action. The reframe is for marketing pages and outreach copy.
  • Same principle Patrick already established for venue-side language ("Founding Venue Profit Pilot," never "auction"). Now extended to the couple side.

9. The couple's personal mood board · Install 2 (Brainstorm Update · May 22)

  • Pinterest-style profile feature. Couples connect Pinterest, paste a board URL, or upload screenshots. The platform extracts the aesthetic into a "vision vector" — palette, textures, recurring motifs (string lights, exposed wood, peonies, marble, candles).
  • Output 1: Venue recommendations re-rank by aesthetic match. Couple who pinned 40 candlelit barn images gets candlelit barn venues first.
  • Output 2: "Your wedding here" AI preview — couple lands on a venue page, the system composites their aesthetic onto the venue's hero photo. The closing moment that makes couples choose Eleventh Vow over a directory.
  • Output 3 · destination match: Same vision vector becomes a trip planner. "You pinned 30 Tuscan vineyard images — here are Tuscan venues in Tuscany, plus Napa, Sonoma, Paso Robles, and Texas Hill Country alternatives if you'd prefer domestic."
  • Why it sticks: couples invest emotional time into curating the mood board, which creates platform lock-in and gives Eleventh Vow priceless preference data.

10. Venue-side reel pipeline · the cold outreach mechanism (Brainstorm Update · May 22)

  • Every target venue gets a custom 10-second cinematic reel generated automatically. Tools: Higgs Field for cinematic camera motion + licensed music sync + scene-clustering AI that ranks photos by composition and emotional content.
  • The reel is the cold outreach asset itself — not a follow-up. The first email leads with the gift. Sample line: "Spent 90 seconds making this for you — imagine couples competing for your June dates after seeing it. We're launching a platform where couples make live offers for venues. Want to be in the first 10?"
  • Watermark the free version with Eleventh Vow. Unwatermarked version unlocks at signup. Even venues that say no often post the reel themselves — watermark = free referral.
  • Phasing: hand-craft the first 5 reels. Build semi-automated pipeline at month 1-2 with manual QA. Full automation by month 3 with quality-gated queue (50/night generated, only top 10 sent).
  • Style picker (Install 2): four variants per venue — Cinematic, Editorial, Romantic, Documentary. Venue picks the one matching their brand. They're emotionally invested in the asset before signing up.

11. Venue activation score · internal only (Brainstorm Update · May 22)

  • Quietly track per-venue promotion behavior: did they post the reel? share their listing? embed the countdown widget on their own site? show up in bidding chat?
  • Top-decile activation venues get featured placement in couple-facing discovery (silent).
  • Bottom-decile venues get coaching emails ("here's how the top venues are promoting").
  • Never surface the score to venues — that creates gaming and resentment. Use it to make better platform decisions without venues feeling scored.
  • Healthy market dynamics: venues that promote get rewarded, which incentivizes more promotion, which gives the platform more brand exposure for free. Venues that won't promote drift down the rankings and eventually churn off — quality flywheel.
