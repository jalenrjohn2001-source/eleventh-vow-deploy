Global Context · The Bigger Picture

The US is the launchpad. The global opportunity is 3-4x larger and growing faster.

The US wedding services market ($68.6B) is real, but it's a fraction of the worldwide opportunity. Asia-Pacific alone accounts for 42% of global wedding services revenue. The destination wedding category — directly addressable by an online auction platform — grows at 14.9% CAGR, more than double the US domestic growth rate.

Strategic positioning · why auctions work BETTER internationally
Analysis · structural advantages by market
International couples physically can't tour the venue before booking. The fixed-price inquiry → wire transfer flow they currently use is actually riskier than an auction with escrow. Eleventh Vow doesn't ask them to give up the tour — they've already given it up. The platform gives them something they didn't have before: competitive price discovery and verified deposit protection. The auction model is structurally stronger in destination markets than domestic.

Operational Constraint · Venue Turnaround

The throughput ceiling — how many auctions a single venue can realistically support.

A venue can't physically host two full-scale weddings 12 hours apart. Industry standard is 12-18 hours minimum between events, 24 hours preferred. This caps how many auctions per venue per month is even possible to fulfill.

Theoretical max events per venue · by day of week mix
Modeled · industry turnaround standards
Reality check: A venue running Saturday-only books ~50/yr. Adding Friday and Sunday increases the ceiling to ~120/yr but turnaround constraints (12+ hrs between events) make true 3-event weekends rare. Adding Tue-Thu weekdays unlocks another ~50 dates with no turnaround conflict (natural weekend buffer). Eleventh Vow's auction inventory should respect this — the realistic auction ceiling per venue is 1.5-2 per month, not 4-5.

The Long-Term Moat · Brainstorm Update

Behavioral data is the asset that compounds against late entrants.

The auction software is reproducible — any competitor can ship it in 3 months. The proprietary asset is the behavioral data on how wedding venue demand actually prices itself. Set up logging from auction #1 because this dataset cannot be retrofitted.

What gets logged per auction
Data schema · Install 1, set up day 1
Venue archetype tag · location · season · day-of-week · starting price · reserve floor · watch-list count at close · unique bidder count · bid velocity curve (every bid timestamped) · final price · time-to-close · couples' mood selections · photo filter usage · traffic source per bidder · weather signals (Saturday weather affects bidding) · reel views / engagement per venue.
Maturity curve: After 100 auctions — usable predictive model. After 1,000 — tell a venue "this date will close at $X with 80% confidence" before they list. After 10,000 — Eleventh Vow is the Zillow of wedding venues, the only company with honest demand-price data on the market. The top 10% (viral bidding wars) and bottom 10% (dud auctions) carry the most signal — optimize logging for outliers, not the median, since outliers are the case studies that fuel marketing AND the negative examples that teach the system what kills demand.
